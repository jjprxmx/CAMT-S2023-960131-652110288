The lips are the visible body part at the mouth of many animals, including humans.
Lips are soft, movable, and serve as the opening for food intake and in the articulation of sound and speech.
Human lips are a tactile sensory organ, and can be an erogenous zone when used in kissing and other acts of intimacy.

The upper and lower lips are referred to as the "Labium superius oris" and "Labium inferius oris"
respectively.The juncture where the lips meet the surrounding skin of the mouth area is the vermilion border,
and the typically reddish area within the borders is called the vermilion zone.
The vermilion border of the upper lip is known as the cupid's bow.
